PLACES TO VISIT

1.Dal Lake

Dal Lake is a famous urban lake in Srinagar, Jammu and Kashmir, known as the "Jewel in the crown of Kashmir" for its scenic beauty, including Mughal gardens, houseboats, and floating gardens. It is a major tourist destination and offers activities like shikara rides, houseboat stays, and visiting floating markets. The lake is divided into four basins by causeways and is surrounded by the Zabarwan mountain range.

Key features and attractions Houseboats: Unique, traditional wooden houseboats are a popular accommodation for tourists. Shikara rides: Visitors can take relaxing rides on these wooden boats to see the lake's sights, like the floating gardens and markets. Floating gardens: Also known as "Rad" in Kashmiri, these are floating islands where lotus flowers bloom in July and August. Mughal gardens: The lake's shoreline is bordered by stunning Mughal-era gardens, including Shalimar Bagh and Nishat Bagh. Floating market: A vibrant market where vendors sell goods from their shikaras, including vegetables, handicrafts, and local delicacies. Char Chinar: A small island in the Lokut Dal basin with four Chinar trees at its corners. Basins: The lake is divided into four basins: Gagribal, Lokut Dal, Bod Dal, and Nageen

2.Vaishno Devi

The Vaishno Devi Temple is a highly sacred Hindu pilgrimage site in India, dedicated to the goddess Vaishno Devi, an incarnation of Goddess Durga. Located in the Trikuta mountains of Jammu and Kashmir, the temple is famous for its devotion to the three-headed rock formations known as "Pindies," representing Goddesses Mahakali, Mahalakshmi, and Saraswati. Devotees undertake a trek of about 12 kilometers from Katra to reach the cave shrine

Location and access Location: The temple is situated in the Trikuta hills, about 13 km from Katra and 63 km from Jammu. How to reach: By air: The nearest airport is Jammu, about 63 km away. By train: The nearest railway station is Katra, which is 13 km from the shrine. By road: The base camp, Katra, is accessible by road. The pilgrimage Trek: Pilgrims must trek approximately 12 km from Katra to reach the cave shrine, which is at an altitude of 5,200 feet. Facilities: The trek route is managed by the Shri Mata Vaishno Devi Shrine Board and provides facilities like accommodation, food stalls, cloakrooms, and medical aid. Duration: The entire journey can take between 12 to 20 hours. Other transport options: For those who cannot walk the entire distance, services like battery-powered cars, horses (ghoda), and palanquins (palki) are available.

3.Gulmerg

Gulmarg is a hill station in the Pir Panjal Range of the Western Himalayas, known as a major center for winter sports and a scenic destination with "meadows of flowers". It is famous for its ski resort, the Gulmarg Gondola (Asia's longest cable car), and the world's highest golf course. The area is popular with tourists for activities like skiing, snowboarding, and sightseeing in all seasons, and features natural beauty with snow-capped mountains and green meadows

Key features Location: A town in the Baramulla district of Jammu and Kashmir, situated in the Pir Panjal range. Etymology: The name "Gulmarg" translates to "Meadow of Flowers". It was formerly known as "Gaurimarg," a name for Goddess Parvati. Altitude: Approximately 8,690 feet (2,650 meters) above sea level. Attractions and activities Gulmarg Gondola: Asia's second-longest and highest cable car, offering stunning panoramic views of the valley. Winter Sports: Known as the "heartland of winter sports," it offers skiing, snowboarding, heli-skiing, and other activities for both beginners and experienced participants. Gulmarg Golf Course: The world's highest golf course. Nature and sightseeing: Enjoy picturesque views of snow-capped peaks, lush meadows, and frozen waterfalls.

4.Patnitop

Patnitop is a popular tourist destination in the Udhampur district of Jammu and Kashmir, known for its natural beauty and adventure activities. Located at an altitude of 2,024 meters in the Shivalik belt of the Himalayas, it features meadows, dense pine forests, and panoramic views of the mountains. Key attractions and activities include trekking, skiing, paragliding, and visiting nearby sites like the Nathatop meadow and the Dr. Syama Prasad Mookerjee Tunnel.

Location and scenery Location: Patnitop is situated on a plateau at an altitude of 2,024 meters along the Jammu-Srinagar National Highway. It is approximately 112 km from Jammu. Natural Beauty: The area is famous for its lush green meadows, dense pine forests, and views of the snow-capped Pir Panjal range. Things to do Adventure Sports: It is a hub for adventure tourism, offering activities like paragliding, skiing, trekking, and horse riding. Sightseeing: Nathatop: A popular spot for paragliding. Kud: A small town famous for the sweet "Patisa" and the Kud Park. Billo Ki Powri: A staircase carved out of rock. Dr. Syama Prasad Mookerjee Tunnel: India's longest road tunnel, located nearby.
